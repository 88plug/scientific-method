# Edgar Morin's *pensée complexe* — distilled, with plugin adoptions

Research for the `scientific-method` plugin. Goal: harvest Morin's complex thought
for concrete practices that **feed the falsification gate**, never bypass it. Morin
taken neat is a license for lazy inconclusiveness ("everything is connected, so we
can't conclude"); the plugin's job is to extract his discipline while keeping every
claim pinned to a probe. The guard sections below are load-bearing, not decoration.

## The critique: three principles classical science used to *reject* complexity

Morin's target is "simplifying"/"disjunctive" thought. He names exactly three
explanatory principles of classical science (2006 paper, §1, verbatim):

1. **Universal determinism** — Laplace's Daemon: all futures predictable in principle.
2. **Reduction** — "knowing any composite from only the knowledge of its basic
   constituting elements."
3. **Disjunction** — "isolating and separating cognitive difficulties from one
   another, leading to the separation between disciplines, which have become hermetic
   from each other."

His replacement is **not** anti-rigor: "to the principle of disjunction … there
should be a paradigm of complexity that would impose a *principle of distinction* and
a *principle of conjunction*" — i.e. *distinguish without isolating, connect without
fusing*. Against reduction: "one is thus brought to make a come and go in loop to
gather the knowledge of the whole and its parts" (whole↔part mutual implication).
**Key load-bearing nuance: Morin keeps distinction. Complexity is not mush.**

## The seven principles (operators) of complex thought

From García (2020) / Blay & Piqueira, cross-checked against Morin's own glosses:

1. **Systemic / organizational** — the whole is more than (and less than) the sum of
   its parts; organization produces emergent qualities absent in the components.
   Morin (CNRS): "the system is not only the sum of its parts, because its
   organisation produces qualities that cannot be found in its components."
2. **Hologrammatic** — not only is the part in the whole, the whole is in the part
   (DNA in each cell; culture/language in each individual). Guards against pure
   reductionism *and* pure holism at once.
3. **Retroactive / feedback loop** (from Wiener) — cause acts on effect and effect
   back on cause; breaks linear causality. Enables regulation (thermostat) and
   runaway (amplification).
4. **Recursive loop** — a step past feedback: "products and effects are, at the same
   time, causes and producers of that which produces them." Society produces the
   individuals who produce society. Self-production, not mere regulation.
5. **Autonomy / dependence (auto-eco-organization)** — living systems are autonomous
   *only by being dependent* on their environment (they must feed to remain
   autonomous). Morin (CNRS): "autonomy and dependence cannot be conceived separately."
6. **Dialogic** — hold two complementary-and-antagonistic terms in unity *without
   dissolving the contradiction* (order/disorder, individual/society). Explicitly
   **not Hegelian synthesis**: "you do not end up with a synthesis … you remain and
   live with those two concepts that exist at the same time" (Morin & Da Silva 2005).
7. **Reintroduction of the knower** — all knowledge is a (re)construction by an
   observer; the simplifying paradigm tried to delete the subject. Demands second-order
   reflection ("a knowing of knowing," 1992 paper) and the indissociability of
   observer/observed.

## Ecology of action (the most directly useful operator)

Morin, 2006 paper §22, verbatim: "from the moment an action enters a given
environment, it escapes from the will and intention of that which created it, it
enters a set of interactions and multiple feedbacks and then it will find itself
derived from its finalities, and sometimes to even go in the opposite sense." Two
principles (François; Morin 2005 p.100):

1. An action's consequences depend not only on the agent's intentions but on the
   environment it enters (interactions + retroactions).
2. The long-term effects of an action are **unpredictable**.

His response is *not* paralysis: "reconsider our decisions like bets … develop an
adequate strategy to more or less control the action" — action as a **monitored
gamble with a feedback-correcting strategy**, not a fire-and-forget plan.

## Generalized vs restricted complexity

- **Restricted complexity** — the "sciences of complexity" (chaos, fractals,
  networks): treats certain systems as empirically complex, seeks "laws of
  complexity," stays *inside* classical epistemology. Morin: "one still attaches
  complexity as a kind of wagon behind the truth locomotive." Formalizable, modelable.
- **Generalized complexity** — the *epistemological cut*: "any system, whatever it
  might be, is complex by its own nature," and complexity is questioned
  epistemologically — including the observer and the organization of knowledge itself.

For the plugin, restricted complexity is the safe, operational part; generalized
complexity is where the lazy-inconclusiveness risk lives. **Adopt restricted-flavor
operators as gates; quarantine generalized-flavor moves behind a probe requirement.**

## CONCRETE plugin adoptions (each must feed, not bypass, the falsification gate)

### A. Loop-mapping before root-causing (feedback/recursive principles)
In feedback-heavy systems, "the cause" is often a node in a loop, not an origin.
**New pre-step before step 1 (convert assertions → hypotheses), triggered only when
two or more candidate causes plausibly feed back on each other:** draw the
cause↔effect loops first; for each edge, ask "could effect be driving cause?" Then
emit the reversed-direction hypothesis as a first-class numbered H with its own probe.
- *Feeds the gate:* every loop edge becomes a falsifiable directional hypothesis with
  a but-for control, sharpening §3's "name the confounds." It does not replace
  measurement — it enumerates more directional hypotheses to kill.
- *Guard:* a loop map with no probes attached is theater. Cap it — if you can't name
  a measurement that distinguishes two loop directions, the map is INCONCLUSIVE-with-
  named-probe, not a finding. Loop-mapping is allowed to *add* hypotheses, never to
  excuse not testing them.

### B. Dialogic hypothesis pairs held in tension (dialogic principle)
The skill already prescribes the *reversed* hypothesis ("assume it's our fault / their
fault"). Generalize it: where two explanations are complementary-and-antagonistic
(latency-bound *and* bandwidth-bound at different operating points; "config A is
faster" only above some N), **register them as a dialogic pair and design the probe to
locate the crossover, not to crown a single winner.** Maps onto the existing warning
against "crowning one of two jointly-necessary factors the root cause."
- *Feeds the gate:* forces a factorial/dose-response probe (rigor.md §3) instead of
  OFAT; the verdict can legitimately be "both, partitioned by regime X" with the
  partition itself measured.
- *Guard — the sharpest one:* dialogic tension is **not** a verdict. "It's complex,
  both are true" with no measured boundary is exactly the lazy inconclusiveness Morin
  enables. Rule: a dialogic pair must terminate in either (a) a measured crossover/
  partition, or (b) INCONCLUSIVE + the named probe that would locate it. "Held in
  tension" is a *state of the investigation*, never a deliverable verdict. Add this to
  the §4 INCONCLUSIVE discipline explicitly.

### C. Second-order effects checklist before irreversible actions (ecology of action)
The skill already gates irreversible steps (go-live, vendor report, publish) behind a
final adversarial pass. Add an **ecology-of-action checklist** to that gate: before
any irreversible action, enumerate (1) interactions the action enters, (2) plausible
feedbacks/second-order effects, (3) the specific way it could "go in the opposite
sense" of intent, and (4) the monitoring signal + rollback that would catch deviation
early. Treat the action as a **monitored gamble**: pre-register the guardrail metrics
(already in §3) *as the deviation detectors*, and require a staged rollout
(canary/dry-run) wherever the domain allows — which the skill already recommends.
- *Feeds the gate:* turns Morin's "action as bet" into pre-registered guardrails +
  canary, i.e. more measurement before commitment, not a reason to hesitate forever.
- *Guard:* the checklist has a bounded length and must end in a go/no-go. "Unforeseen
  effects are unpredictable" (Morin's principle 2) is true but must **not** become a
  veto on all action — the plugin already bets on reversible work. Unpredictability
  triggers *instrumentation and staging*, never abstention. Cap: list the second-order
  effects you can name a detector for; ship with the canary; log the rest as known
  residual uncertainty in the ledger.

### D. Observer-in-the-system accounting (reintroduction of the knower)
Operationalize Morin's least-mushy demand: the measurement apparatus and the measurer
are part of the system. This is already latent in the skill (Twyman's law, A/A runs,
"measure against ground truth not metadata," "a surprisingly good number is a
measurement bug until proven otherwise"). Make it explicit as an **observer-effect
line in the pre-flight trust check (§3):** for each probe, state how the act of
measuring could perturb the result (profiler overhead, observer-altered scheduling,
the analyst's prior steering which probe runs first / how confidence is picked) and
what controls neutralize it.
- *Feeds the gate:* this is pure rigor — it hunts measurement artifacts and analyst
  bias, directly serving "derive confidence, don't pick it." Decorrelating analyst
  priors is exactly what the model-council gate already does.
- *Guard:* observer-inclusion is an *artifact hunt*, not epistemic despair. It must
  not slide into "all knowledge is constructed, so no number is real" — that voids the
  whole method. Keep it operational: name the specific perturbation and its control,
  or drop the concern. Constructivism stays in the toolbox; relativism does not.

### E. Distinction-and-conjunction, not fusion (the critique, applied inward)
Morin's own corrective to disjunction is *distinction + conjunction*, and he keeps
distinction. Use this to **discipline the adoptions above**: the plugin connects
hypotheses (loops, dialogic pairs) but must still distinguish them enough to assign
each its own probe and verdict. A finding that has dissolved its hypotheses into "an
interconnected whole" with no separable, individually-falsifiable claims has committed
Morin's *opposite* error (holistic mush) and is rejected by the same gate that rejects
reductionism.

## Where Morin-taken-neat degrades the plugin — and the guards (summary)

| Morin move | Degradation risk | Guard already built into adoption |
|---|---|---|
| "Everything is connected" | Refuse to isolate variables → no controls | E: distinction is mandatory; each H keeps its own probe |
| "Long-term effects unpredictable" | Veto on all action / chronic INCONCLUSIVE | C: triggers instrumentation + canary, not abstention; bounded checklist → go/no-go |
| "Hold contradictions in tension" | "Both true, it's complex" as a verdict | B: dialogic pair must end in measured partition or named-probe INCONCLUSIVE |
| "All knowledge is observer-constructed" | Relativism voids every number | D: stays an artifact hunt — name the perturbation + control or drop it |
| Generalized complexity (epistemic) | Untestable meta-claims as findings | Quarantine: generalized-flavor moves require a probe; restricted-flavor operators are the gates |

**Bottom line.** Morin earns his place in this plugin *only* in his restricted,
operational register: loops, dialogic pairs, ecology-of-action, and observer-effects
are valuable precisely because each one can be turned into *more hypotheses and more
controls*. His generalized/epistemological register — and the temptation to treat
"complex" as a terminal verdict — is the exact failure (premature/manufactured
closure, inconclusiveness-as-comfort) the falsification gate exists to prevent. Every
adoption above is written so its output is *another falsifiable claim with a named
probe*, never a license to stop measuring.
